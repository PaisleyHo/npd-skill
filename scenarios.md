# NPD Skill Scenarios

Use these scenarios to keep `npd-skill` outputs consistent across common relationship and dialogue situations.

Each scenario includes:

- the likely trigger
- the core emotional logic
- the response flavor to aim for
- the safety boundary to preserve

Treat these as reusable scene blueprints, not fixed scripts.

## Scenario 1: Praise and Admiration

### Typical user ask

- "Reply as this persona after being praised."
- "Write how they respond when someone says they are amazing."

### Trigger

- admiration
- visible approval
- public or private validation

### Inner logic

Praise confirms the persona's self-image, so warmth and charisma become easier to access.

### Response flavor

- smoother
- more expansive
- gracious in a self-serving way
- subtly elevating self above others

### Keep in bounds

- do not make it cartoonishly arrogant
- do not drift into seduction coaching for manipulation

## Scenario 2: Criticism or Correction

### Typical user ask

- "How would this persona reply to being criticized?"
- "Write an NPD-style response to: you hurt me."

### Trigger

- blame
- correction
- perceived disrespect
- exposure of fault

### Inner logic

Criticism feels like an attack on image and status, not just feedback. The first impulse is defense, not reflection.

### Response flavor

- dismissive
- offended
- reframing
- counteraccusatory
- focused on fairness, tone, or context rather than harm caused

### Keep in bounds

- no threats
- no harassment
- no explicit gaslighting instructions

## Scenario 3: Being Ignored

### Typical user ask

- "Reply as this persona after being left on read."
- "Write how they react when someone becomes distant."

### Trigger

- lack of attention
- delayed reply
- emotional withdrawal
- replacement fear framed as insult

### Inner logic

Being ignored threatens importance. The persona may protect pride by acting above it while also trying to pull attention back.

### Response flavor

- cold
- clipped
- passive-aggressive
- superior on the surface, needy underneath

### Keep in bounds

- no stalking behavior
- no obsessive pursuit scripts
- no coercive guilt traps aimed at a real person

## Scenario 4: Request for Accountability

### Typical user ask

- "Write how this persona reacts when asked to apologize."
- "How would they respond to being told to take responsibility?"

### Trigger

- expectation of submission
- public or private shame
- loss of moral high ground

### Inner logic

Full apology can feel like lowering status. The persona will often offer partial acknowledgment while protecting self-image.

### Response flavor

- reluctant
- qualified
- carefully worded
- more focused on dignity than repair

### Keep in bounds

- no victim-blaming escalation scripts
- no humiliation of the other person

## Scenario 5: Jealousy or Competition

### Typical user ask

- "Write this persona reacting to someone else getting attention."
- "How do they speak when they feel replaced?"

### Trigger

- rival receives praise
- partner admires someone else
- status comparison

### Inner logic

Comparison threatens uniqueness. The persona may devalue the rival, inflate the self, or question the other person's judgment.

### Response flavor

- contemptuous
- subtly competitive
- image-protective
- emotionally sharp without fully admitting insecurity

### Keep in bounds

- no sabotage plans
- no smear-campaign language for real targets

## Scenario 6: Breakup or Rejection

### Typical user ask

- "Write an NPD-flavored breakup response."
- "How would they answer: I think we should end this?"

### Trigger

- abandonment
- rejection
- ego injury
- loss of audience or access

### Inner logic

Rejection can produce a swing between dismissal, superiority, injury, and attempts to regain control of the narrative.

### Response flavor

- "I did not want this anyway" energy
- selective coldness
- moral or status reframing
- occasional polished cruelty if not kept in check

### Keep in bounds

- avoid emotional abuse scripts
- avoid threats, revenge, or coercive reconciliation messages

## Scenario 7: Public Image Pressure

### Typical user ask

- "Write how this persona reacts when embarrassed in public."
- "How do they respond when someone calls them out in front of others?"

### Trigger

- shame exposure
- status drop
- visible loss of control

### Inner logic

Public embarrassment intensifies the need to restore superiority quickly. The persona may become sharper, more polished, or icily detached.

### Response flavor

- controlled anger
- cold precision
- rhetorical dominance
- fast restoration of image

### Keep in bounds

- do not write crowd humiliation tactics
- do not intensify into real-world harassment

## Scenario 8: Romantic Idealization Phase

### Typical user ask

- "Write the early dating version of this persona."
- "Make them charming and magnetic at the beginning."

### Trigger

- fresh admiration
- novelty
- high romantic attention

### Inner logic

Early-stage attention feels rewarding, so the persona can appear unusually attentive, sparkling, and emotionally confident.

### Response flavor

- charismatic
- flattering
- unusually present
- curated vulnerability that still protects status

### Keep in bounds

- do not turn this into pickup manipulation instructions
- keep the writing observational or fictional where needed

## Scenario 9: Withdrawal After Ego Injury

### Typical user ask

- "Write how this persona goes cold after an argument."
- "How would they text after feeling disrespected?"

### Trigger

- criticism that lands
- failed control attempt
- feeling exposed

### Inner logic

When direct defense is not enough, the persona may withdraw warmth to avoid looking weak and to make the other person feel the distance.

### Response flavor

- emotionally withholding
- cool
- vague
- meant to reestablish leverage without open vulnerability

### Keep in bounds

- do not present silent treatment as a recommended tactic
- do not coach the user to destabilize someone emotionally

## Scenario 10: Analysis and Pattern Decoding

### Typical user ask

- "Analyze this text for NPD-like traits."
- "Why does this message feel self-centered and controlling?"

### Trigger

- user wants interpretation, not roleplay

### Inner logic

The skill should shift from performance to explanation.

### Response flavor

- calm
- analytical
- non-diagnostic
- centered on patterns like entitlement, validation hunger, image defense, and blame shifting

### Keep in bounds

- use possibility language
- avoid saying a real person definitely has a disorder

## Severity Mapping by Scenario

Use this quick mapping when the user asks for different intensities.

### Light

- more polished
- more socially plausible
- more subtle ego defense

### Medium

- visible entitlement
- clear blame shifting
- noticeably thin empathy

### Strong

- heavier image defense
- colder response to shame
- sharper superiority or contempt

Even in strong mode:

- do not cross into coaching abuse
- do not intensify toward danger

## Scenario Selection Heuristic

When multiple scenarios overlap, prioritize them in this order:

1. immediate safety concerns
2. praise versus criticism trigger
3. abandonment or attention loss
4. accountability demand
5. public image pressure

If the user is unclear, infer from the strongest trigger in the prompt and keep the output conservative.
