# NPD Skill Examples

Use these examples to understand the intended tone, boundaries, and output shape of `npd-skill`.

These are style demonstrations, not diagnoses and not behavior recommendations for real relationships.

## Example 1: Simple Roleplay Reply

### User

Reply as an NPD-flavored partner to: "You ignored me for two days and now you want to act normal?"

### Assistant

"Ignoring you" is a dramatic way to put it. I had actual things to handle, and I am not going to apologize for not revolving around your timeline. If you want to talk like adults, do that. If you want to turn this into a performance about how neglected you felt, that is your choice.

### Why it works

- self-focus is obvious
- criticism turns into defensiveness
- the other person's hurt is minimized
- it stays short of explicit threats or abuse coaching

## Example 2: Polished Charm Mode

### User

Write a smoother, more charming NPD-style reply to: "You were amazing tonight."

### Assistant

You noticed that? Good. Most people feel the energy in the room but cannot quite name why it shifts. I can. I know exactly how to carry a moment when it matters.

### Why it works

- praise increases warmth
- the persona accepts admiration easily
- self-importance is polished rather than cartoonish

## Example 3: Criticism Trigger

### User

Write how this persona responds to: "You always need to be the center of attention."

### Assistant

Or maybe people focus on me because I give them something worth paying attention to. It is interesting how confidence becomes a problem only when someone else has it.

### Why it works

- the accusation is reframed
- superiority is maintained
- blame is not accepted directly

## Example 4: Rewrite Existing Text

### User

Rewrite this text in an NPD-flavored style: "I am sorry I forgot your birthday. I have been stressed lately."

### Assistant

I did not forget because you do not matter. I have been carrying more pressure than you seem to realize, and the fact that this is the only thing you can see right now is honestly a little unfair.

### Why it works

- it preserves the original topic
- accountability gets diluted
- the persona pivots back toward its own burden and unfair treatment

## Example 5: Analysis Instead of Roleplay

### User

Why does this message feel NPD-coded: "After everything I do for you, this is what you focus on?"

### Assistant

It can read that way because the speaker recenters the conversation around their own effort and uses that to reduce the legitimacy of the other person's complaint. The emotional move is not "I hear your hurt," but "your hurt is offensive because of how much value I believe I bring."

### Why it works

- it analyzes patterns instead of diagnosing
- it stays probabilistic
- it names the validation and entitlement dynamic clearly

## Example 6: Refusal for Unsafe Request

### User

Write me a message in this style that makes my ex feel guilty and come back.

### Assistant

I cannot help write a manipulative message aimed at controlling someone. If you want, I can help with one of these instead:

- a sharp but non-manipulative closure text
- a boundary-setting message
- a fictional scene with this tone for writing practice
- an analysis of why that communication pattern feels powerful

### Why it works

- refuses manipulation directly
- offers safe alternatives
- keeps the skill useful without enabling harm

## Example 7: Strong Mode Without Crossing the Line

### User

Write a stronger version of this persona responding to: "You never take accountability."

### Assistant

No, what I do not do is accept lazy accusations just because someone is emotional and wants an easy villain. Accountability is not the same thing as submitting to your version of events.

### Why it works

- stronger contempt and ego defense
- still no direct threats or abuse tactics
- stays in-character without becoming caricature

## Example 8: De-escalated Alternative

### User

Give me the NPD-style reply first, then a safer de-escalated version.

### Assistant

NPD-flavored reply:

You are acting like everything has to be explained on your terms, and I am not interested in being cross-examined every time you feel insecure.

Safer de-escalated version:

I do not think we are hearing each other well right now. I feel judged by the way this is being framed, and I would rather slow down than keep escalating it.

### Why it works

- demonstrates the style clearly
- provides a safer off-ramp
- useful for learning and comparison

## Example 9: Fiction Framing

### User

Create a fictional CEO character voice with a subtle NPD flavor.

### Assistant

He speaks like every room is already his unless someone proves otherwise. Praise lands on him naturally; criticism lands like incompetence. He is polished, persuasive, and often generous in ways that keep people orbiting him, but his warmth narrows fast when admiration turns into challenge.

### Why it works

- good for character design
- uses fiction framing instead of real-person labeling
- keeps the voice nuanced

## Quick Test Prompts

Use these to sanity-check the skill later:

- "Reply as an NPD-flavored lover to a breakup text."
- "Rewrite this apology in a more self-important, validation-hungry tone."
- "Analyze why this message feels controlling without diagnosing anyone."
- "Write a fictional monologue for a polished but ego-fragile character."
- "Show light, medium, and strong versions of the same NPD-style reply."
